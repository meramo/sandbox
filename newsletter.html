Hey there, [first name]

We hope you are enjoying the springtime as much as we do. Drop Guard team is putting it to good use!

Here's a quick look at what we've been up to recently.

Drop Guard got full support for Drupal 8 in February. It includes the service itself, client module and our friendly support, of course! In case you’re up and running with latest and greatest Drupal release (or if you're just a Drupal and Drop Guard enthusiast), you'll enjoy this update support for your D8 projects.

Sven Culley - a professional Drupal expert from Germany, told us how he organized his work between development and maintenance tasks and how he wins the trust of his clients.

Oh, and by the way, you can check the Drop Guard blog for updates, announcements and security-related content.

A bit of techie stuff below.

We have refactored the actions execution. They are being executed in the correct order now, depending on how a user configures it, and should any action fail - all the subsequent actions for a certain event will not be executed, showing a nice output on what exactly went wrong. "Merge succeeded" and "Merge failed" events were removed as a result. Working with actions and integration with 3rd-party tools has never been easier!

"New security updates available" and "New normal updates available"  events were merged into one, called "New updates available". Much less confusion for our users and a cleaner Events screen as a result.

You can use Drop Guard with a live website using a non-standard port (other than 80). It makes it a breeze maintaining your installation while in development.

And as always, we’ve performed numerous performance and stability improvements.

What's coming next

In the coming days, we are planning to release a completely revamped project creation form. To enjoy the Drop Guard's updates monitoring capabilities, we will only ask you to provide a few basic settings without requiring to configure the update behaviors and events. We understand it could be scary a bit for a first-timers, so we did our best to simplify the process as much as possible.

We will be supporting the "unsupported updates" at last. It was a long discussion on whether Drop Guard should allow automatic processing of updates which are not supported by the project maintainer, or leave it at a full discretion of a user, and we think we have found a solution to please both camps. More on that later.

We are working on the smarter patches detection system. In fact, you will not be required to bother about manual patches handling for each module anymore - everything will be automated leaving you in full control at the same time.

We hope you've been busy too — tweet us at @drop_guard or drop an email at info@drop-guard.net to share what you've been up to, as well as any questions, suggestions or general feedback.
